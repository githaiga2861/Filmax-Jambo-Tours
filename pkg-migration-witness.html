The Journey

The Greatest Wildlife Spectacle on Earth

Between July and October, approximately 1.5 million wildebeest, 400,000 zebra and 200,000 Thomson's gazelle cross the Mara River. What happens at those crossings — the crocodiles, the panic, the courage, the drowning and the surviving — is the single most dramatic wildlife event on earth.

Migration Witness positions you at the river in total luxury, with a dedicated crossing spotter whose sole purpose is to be at the right place at the right time. When the crossing happens, you are there. In a private camp with your own guide, your own vehicle, and a dawn balloon flight to see the migration herds from above.

Day by Day

Your Itinerary

Day 01

Nairobi → Private Mara River Camp

Wilson Airport · Mara airstrip

Charter flight to the Mara. Your private riverside camp is positioned within walking distance of the most active crossing points — chosen for this purpose above all others. Your dedicated crossing spotter briefs you over lunch: where the herds are, which crossing point is "live," what to expect when it happens.

  • Charter flight
  • Spotter briefing lunch
  • Afternoon river watch
  • Camp riverside dinner
Day 02

Balloon Dawn + River Vigil

Mara sky · Mara River

Pre-dawn. Your balloon lifts off as the first light touches the horizon below you. From 500 metres, the migration herds are visible as a living, breathing carpet across the plains — vast columns converging on the river. Post-flight champagne breakfast. Then straight to the crossing point. Your spotter has been there since 5am.

  • Pre-dawn balloon launch
  • 1-hour flight over migration herds
  • Champagne bush breakfast
  • River crossing vigil
Day 03

Full River Crossing Day

Mara River Crossing Points

An entire day at the river. Crossings can happen at any moment — and when they do, they happen fast. The build-up is as compelling as the event itself: the herds massing on the Tanzania side, the lead animals approaching the bank and retreating, the tension rising, and then — the commitment. The chaos, the crocodiles, the wildebeest swimming with everything they have. Witnessing your first crossing is a moment you will not describe for years without emotion.

  • Full-day river vigil
  • Crossing documentation with guide
  • In-field lunch at river
  • Evening camp debrief
Day 04

Plains Safari + Second Crossing Attempt

Maasai Mara Open Plains

A morning on the open plains for lion, cheetah, and elephant encounters away from the river. The predators are superabundant during migration — opportunistic lions follow the herds. Afternoon return to the river for a second crossing vigil. Fine dinner under canvas as the Mara sky fills with stars.

  • Plains big cat drive
  • Cheetah and lion encounters
  • Afternoon river crossing vigil
  • Gourmet camp dinner
Day 05

Final Morning → Departure

Mara River · Charter Nairobi

One final morning at the river. Breakfast at camp with the sound of the wildebeest in the distance. Charter to Nairobi with a migration memory that will outlast everything.

  • Final morning river drive
  • Camp breakfast
  • Charter to Nairobi
  • Departure transfer
