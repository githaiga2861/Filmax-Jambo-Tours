The Journey

Ancient Swahili Culture, White Coral Shores

Lamu is a world apart. The oldest continuously inhabited settlement in East Africa — a UNESCO World Heritage Site — Lamu Old Town has no cars, its narrow coral-stone lanes unchanged for eight centuries. Donkeys carry goods. Dhows sail by the same stars that guided Arab merchants in the 9th century.

The Lamu Archipelago offers a Kenya that has nothing to do with safari — an intimate encounter with one of the world's great living cultures, set against the backdrop of the Indian Ocean's most perfect islands. White coral beaches, dhow sailing, octopus grilled on open fires, and a silence that is the rarest luxury of all.

Day by Day

Your Itinerary

Day 01

Nairobi → Lamu

Wilson Airport · Lamu airstrip · Dhow transfer

Charter flight from Nairobi to Lamu airstrip on Manda Island, then the traditional dhow water crossing to Lamu Island itself — your first encounter with this extraordinary place. A porter navigates your bags through the narrow alleys to your heritage hotel. Afternoon walk through Lamu Old Town with a local guide.

  • Charter flight
  • Dhow water crossing
  • Heritage hotel arrival
  • Lamu Old Town walk
Day 02

Lamu Old Town Deep Dive

Lamu Island

Lamu Fort, the Lamu Museum, the Friday Mosque — the layers of Swahili civilization revealed by your knowledgeable local guide. Lunch at a rooftop restaurant above the waterfront. Afternoon at leisure: the famous Shela Beach, white sand stretching 12km, is a 20-minute walk from the Old Town.

  • Lamu Fort visit
  • Museum tour
  • Waterfront lunch
  • Shela Beach afternoon
Day 03

Manda Island · Dhow Sail

Manda Channel · Indian Ocean

Private dhow for the day. Sail to Manda Island's uninhabited beaches — white coral sand, palm fronds, complete solitude. Snorkel the coral reef off Manda Toto island. The dhow crew prepare a fresh seafood lunch on the beach: caught this morning, grilled over charcoal in the shade of a palapa. Return at sunset with the sky turning every shade of fire.

  • Private dhow charter
  • Manda beach landing
  • Coral snorkel
  • Beach seafood lunch
  • Sunset return
Day 04

Swahili Cooking + Free Day

Lamu Island

Morning Swahili cooking masterclass with a celebrated local chef — learning to prepare pilau, biryani, coconut fish curry and the famous Lamu sambuza. The afternoon is yours completely: hammock, book, swimming from the waterfront, or exploring the alleyways further.

  • Swahili cooking class
  • Market ingredient tour
  • Lunch of your cooking
  • Free afternoon
Day 05

Final Morning → Departure

Lamu · Charter Nairobi

One final coffee on the hotel rooftop above the dhow harbour. Dhow water transfer to Lamu airstrip, charter to Nairobi. Lamu leaves its imprint differently from safari — quieter, deeper, longer lasting.

  • Final morning at leisure
  • Dhow to airstrip
  • Charter to Nairobi
  • Transfer assistance
