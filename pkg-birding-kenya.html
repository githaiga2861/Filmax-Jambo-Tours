The Journey

Over 1,100 Species. Africa's Premier Birding.

Kenya records more bird species than almost any country on earth — over 1,100 confirmed species, from the towering ostrich to the jewel-bright sunbird. Its diversity of habitats — alpine moorland, tropical rainforest, rift valley lakes, open savannah and Indian Ocean coast — produces a birding list that serious birders dream about for years before their first visit.

The Birding Kenya itinerary moves through three distinct ecosystems: Lake Nakuru for waterbirds and flamingos; Kakamega Forest for the rare western rainforest species found nowhere else in Kenya; and the Maasai Mara for savannah specialists and raptors. Led by a fellow of the African Bird Club with a life list exceeding 3,000 species.

Day by Day

Your Itinerary

Day 01

Nairobi → Lake Nakuru

Transfer via Naivasha · Lake Nakuru NP

Depart Nairobi west, stopping at Lake Naivasha en route — a productive wetland for pelicans, cormorants, African fish eagles, and the sought-after African jacana. Continue to Lake Nakuru. Afternoon birding walk along the lake shore as thousands of flamingos paint the horizon pink.

  • Naivasha wetland stop
  • Flamingo lake walk
  • Pelican roost visit
  • Acacia woodland birding
Day 02

Full Lake Nakuru Day

Lake Nakuru National Park

Full day at Nakuru. The lake's alkalinity fluctuates year to year, sometimes hosting a million flamingos, sometimes fewer — but always spectacular. The park is also home to the rare Hemprich's hornbill, African crowned eagle, Jackson's widowbird, and Hildebrandt's starling. Baboon Cliff for an aerial perspective of the flamingo spectacle.

  • Dawn waterbird survey
  • Flamingo count session
  • Raptor cliff watching
  • Forest birding afternoon
Day 03

Nakuru → Kakamega Forest

Western Kenya · Kakamega Forest

Drive west to Kakamega — Kenya's only tropical rainforest, a relic of the Congo Basin forest that once stretched across equatorial Africa. It is home to over 350 bird species including 15 found nowhere else in East Africa: Turner's Eremomela, the Chapin's flycatcher, African shrike-flycatcher, and the extraordinary Great Blue Turaco.

  • Road transfer west
  • Forest arrival birding walk
  • Sunset raptor watch
  • Checklist review dinner
Day 04

Full Kakamega Day

Kakamega Forest Reserve

An entire day inside one of Africa's most biodiverse patches of forest. Dawn chorus at 5:30am — the dawn chorus in tropical rainforest is among the most extraordinary sounds in nature. Your guide locates speciality species systematically through the morning. Afternoon forest walk along the Isukha trail for understorey species.

  • Pre-dawn chorus walk
  • Great Blue Turaco session
  • Understorey specialists
  • Evening owl search
Day 05

Kakamega → Maasai Mara

Eastern transfer · Mara ecosystem

Transfer from Kakamega to the Mara — a full half-day drive that passes through the rift valley and several distinct habitats, each producing new species. Arrive in the Mara for afternoon savannah birding: the Mara hosts over 450 species including 57 raptors.

  • Transfer birding stops
  • Rift Valley viewpoint stop
  • Mara savannah arrival
  • Raptor evening drive
Day 06

Full Mara Birding Day → Nairobi

Maasai Mara · Return

A final morning dedicated to Mara savannah specialists: the Secretary Bird striding across open plains, Martial Eagle on a dead tree, Kori Bustard displaying, and the extraordinary Lilac-breasted Roller catching the Mara morning light in its full iridescent glory. Return to Nairobi via road, arriving late afternoon.

  • Dawn raptor drive
  • Secretary bird tracking
  • Roller and bustard session
  • Nairobi return
