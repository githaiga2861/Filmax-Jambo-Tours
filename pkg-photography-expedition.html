The Journey

For Those Who Hunt with a Lens

Wildlife photography is not about equipment — it is about knowledge, patience, and positioning. The Photography Expedition pairs a professional wildlife photographer (whose work appears in National Geographic, BBC Wildlife, and GEO) with the most meticulously outfitted safari vehicle in Kenya.

Maximum four guests. Custom camera rigs on every window position. Bean bags, gimbal mounts, and a vehicle fitted with a raised hide platform. The photographer manages the light; your guide manages the wildlife. Post-processing sessions each evening cover RAW workflow, composition principles, and species identification. You will return home with images you could not have made anywhere else.

Day by Day

Your Itinerary

Day 01

Nairobi → Maasai Mara · Equipment Orientation

Wilson Airport · Mara Camp

Charter to the Mara. Late afternoon equipment orientation with your lead photographer: camera rig setup, bean bag technique, understanding your specific camera system's limitations and strengths in low light. Brief sunset drive to calibrate.

  • Charter flight
  • Camera rig setup
  • Photographer briefing
  • Calibration drive
Day 02

Mara Dawn: Big Cats in First Light

Maasai Mara · Dawn position

Pre-dawn departure to a predetermined position where lion are most likely. First light — the "golden hour" of wildlife photography — lasts approximately 40 minutes and produces the images that define safari photography. Your photographer has positioned the vehicle for optimal east-facing morning light. Post-session review at camp.

  • Pre-dawn departure
  • Big cat first light session
  • Image review at camp
  • Afternoon cheetah session
Day 03

Mara: Birds, Landscapes & Action

Maasai Mara · Mara River

A day covering the full range of Mara subjects: the river's hippos and crocodiles for action photography; the vast landscape compositions available from Rhino Ridge; bird photography in the acacia forest at camp. Evening post-processing session covering RAW workflow in Lightroom.

  • River action photography
  • Landscape composition session
  • Bird forest session
  • Lightroom RAW evening workshop
Day 04

Mara: Migration Herds & Open Plains

Maasai Mara

Focus on the wildebeest and zebra herds that define Mara photography. Dust, movement, chaos — and within it, moments of extraordinary stillness. Your photographer guides you on capturing both the scale of the herds and the intimacy of individual animals. Backlit late afternoon driving session for silhouette work.

  • Herd photography
  • Dust and movement session
  • Backlit silhouette drive
  • Final Mara evening
Day 05

Mara → Amboseli

Charter south · Kilimanjaro framing

Charter to Amboseli. The photographic priority shifts to the iconic: elephant + Kilimanjaro compositions that have defined the image of Africa for a generation. Your photographer knows every angle and time of day for this singular shot.

  • Charter flight
  • Kilimanjaro orientation
  • Elephant arrival afternoon
  • Mountain evening session
Day 06

Full Amboseli Photography Day

Amboseli National Park

The clearest Kilimanjaro views come before 8am, after which cloud usually builds. Pre-dawn departure. Elephant herds move against the mountain background; bull elephants with sweeping tusks in dust clouds. Evening: cheetah on the open plain in last light. Final post-processing workshop covering portfolio editing and submission-quality exports.

  • Pre-dawn Kilimanjaro session
  • Elephant and mountain drives
  • Cheetah in last light
  • Portfolio editing workshop
Day 07

Final Amboseli Dawn → Nairobi

Amboseli · Wilson Airport

One last dawn session — by this point your eye has changed. The way you read light, anticipate animal behaviour, and manage your equipment is fundamentally different from a week ago. Charter to Nairobi with a card full of images and a vision that has been permanently expanded.

  • Final dawn session
  • Review and print selection
  • Charter to Nairobi
  • Departure
